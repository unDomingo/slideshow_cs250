## CS 250 Module Eight Journal

### Interpreting User Needs
To interpret user needs, I focus on understanding what the user is trying to accomplish rather than just the technical requirements. Breaking down requirements into smaller pieces, like user stories, helps clarify what features are actually needed. User stories allow me to think from the user's perspective and ensure the program solves a real problem instead of just meeting technical specifications.

### Approach to Developing Programs
My approach to developing programs is based on Agile principles, especially flexibility and continuous improvement. I prefer to work in small iterations, testing features as I build them instead of waiting until the end. Practices like sprint planning, daily check-ins (even if informal), and retrospectives help me stay organized and adjust when something is not working. I have learned that responding to change is often more important than strictly following a plan.

### Being a Good Team Member
Being a good team member in software development means communicating clearly, being reliable, and supporting others when needed. It also means being open to feedback and willing to adapt. I think it's important to share progress regularly, help remove blockers, and contribute to a positive team environment. Good teamwork ensures that the final product is stronger and more aligned with user needs.
